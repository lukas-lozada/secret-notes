  4. A shop manufactures performance bikes. The manager estimates that the total cost (in dollars) of producing b bikes is: $$C\left( b \right) = 1200 + 25b - 0.14{b^2}$$

    1. Average Cost: Find the average cost function, $\bar C\left( b \right)$. Calculate $\bar C\left( 7 \right)$ and interpret the results.

      $\bar C\left( b \right)\, = \frac{{1200 + 25b - 0.14{b^2}}}{b}$

      $\bar C\left( b \right)\, = \frac{{1200}}{b} + 25 - 0.14b$

      $\bar C\left( 7 \right)\, = \frac{{1200}}{7} + 25 - 0.14(7) = 195.45$

      The average cost of producing 7 bikes is \$195.45.

    2. Marginal Cost: Find the Marginal Cost Function,$ C'\left( b \right)$. Calculate $ {C'} \left( 7 \right)$ and interpret the results.

      $ C\left( b \right)\, = 1200 + 25b - 0.14{b^2}$

      ${C ^\prime }\left( b \right) = 25-0.28b$

      ${ C ^\prime }\left( 7 \right) = 23.04$

      When the 8th bike is produced, the marginal cost is \$23.04.

    3. Explain the difference between parts a and b.

      On average, the cost to produce each of the 7 bikes is \$195.45. The cost to produce the 8th bike is approximately \$23.04.

  5. You are the manager of Sassy Surf Creations, a new trend-setting clothing manufacturer. The cost function for your very exclusive Tai Kwon Do Dragon T-shirts is $$C\left( x \right)=0.02{{x}^{2}}+7.5x+600$$ in dollars, and you sell the shirts for $\$20.00$ each. Determine the following.

    1. The revenue function

      $R(x)=20.00x$



    2. The marginal revenue function

      ${R}'\left( x \right)=20.00$



    3. The profit function

      $P(x)=R(x)-C(x)$

      $P(x)=20.00x-\left( 0.02{{x}^{2}}+7.50x+600 \right)$

      $P(x)=20.00x-0.02{{x}^{2}}-7.50x-600$

      $P(x)=-0.02{{x}^{2}}+12.50x-600$



    4. The marginal profit function

      $P'\left( x \right)=-0.04x+12.50$



    5. The marginal revenue and marginal profit for the sale of 300 T-shirts

      ${R}'\left( 300 \right)=\$20.00$

      ${P}'\left(300\right)=-0.04\left(300\right)+12.50=\$0.50$



    6. Explain what marginal revenue and profit mean, in general.

      At a specific production level, the revenue and profit generated if one more is produced and sold.



    7. Explain what the marginal revenue and marginal profit mean for the sale of 300 T-shirts.

      The revenue for the 301st T-shirt is \$$20.00$. The profit for the 301st T-shirt is \$$0.50.$

  6. The total cost, in dollars, of operating a factory that produces gourmet gas ranges is $$C(x)=0.5{{x}^{2}}+40x+8000,$$ where x is the number of gas ranges produced.

    1. Determine the marginal cost of producing 5000 gas ranges and compare this with the actual cost of producing the 5001st gas range.

      ${C}'\left( x \right)=x+40$

      ${C}'\left( 5000 \right)=5000+40=\$5040$ Estimated cost to produce 5001st gas range

      The actual cost to produce the 5001st gas range is $C\left( 5001 \right)-C\left( 5000 \right).$

      $C(5001)=\$12,713,040.50$

      $C(5000)=\$12,708,000.00$

      $C\left( 5001 \right)-C\left( 5000 \right)=5040.50$

      Actual cost to produce 5001st gas range is \$$5040.50.$



    2. Determine the average cost of producing 5000 gas ranges. Compare this value to those in part a. What do you notice?

      $\overline{C}(x)=\frac{0.5{{x}^{2}}+40x+8000}{x}=0.5x+40+\frac{8000}{x}$

      $\overline{C}(5000)=0.5(5000)+40+\frac{8000}{5000}=\$2541.60$

      The average cost is significantly less than the cost of producing the 5001st gas range.

  7. The cost, in dollars, of producing x hot tubs is modeled by $C(x)=3450x-1.02{{x}^{2}},$ when the company produces up to 1500 hot tubs.

    ${C}'\left( x \right)=3450-2.04x$

    1. Determine the marginal cost when 750 hot tubs are made. What does this mean to the manufacturer?

      ${C}'(750)=3450-2.04(750)=\$1920$

      The estimated cost to produce the 751st hot tub is \$1920.



    2. Find the cost of producing the 751st hot tub.

      $C(751)-C(750)=2015668.98-2013750 =\$1918.98$

      The actual cost to produce the 751st hot tub is \$1918.98.



    3. Compare and comment on the values in parts a and b.

      Marginal cost (a) is an approximation for the cost of the next item. The actual cost (b) is the exact cost according to the model (not an approximation).



    4. Each hot tub is sold for $9200. Write a revenue function for the sale of x hot tubs.

      $R(x)=9200x$



    5. Determine the rate of change of the profit for the sale of 750 hot tubs.

      $P(x)=R(x)-C(x)=9200x-(3450x-1.02{{x}^{2}})$

      $P(x) =1.02{{x}^{2}}+5750x$

      $P'(x)=2.04x+5750$

      $P'(750)=2.04(750)+5750=\$7280$

      Profit is increasing by \$7280 for each hot tub, when 750 are produced.

  8. The National Honor Society at a local high school sells T-shirts for its yearly fundraiser. The cost of producing the x shirts is $$C(x)=-0.0005{{x}^{2}}+7.5x+200,$$ and each shirt sells for \$$15.00$.

    1. Determine the revenue function.

      $R(x)=15x$



    2. Determine the marginal cost function.

      $C'(x)=-0.0010x+7.5$



    3. Determine the marginal revenue function.

      $R'(x)=\$15$

      Revenue increases by \$15 for each T-shirt sold.



    4. Determine the marginal cost AND the marginal revenue when 1500 shirts are sold.

      $C'(1500)=-0.0010\left( 1500 \right)+7.5=\$6$

      The cost to produce the 1501st shirt is \$6.

      ${R}'\left(1500\right)=\$15$

      The revenue for the 1501st shirt is \$15.



    5. Determine the ACTUAL cost of producing the 1501st shirt, according to the model.

      $C( 1501)-C( 1500)$

      $=\left( -0.0005{{(1501)}^{2}}+7.5(1501)+200 \right)-\left( -0.0005{{(1500)}^{2}}+7.5(1500)+200 \right)$

      $= 10330.995-10325=10330.995-10325=5.995$

      The actual cost to produce the 1501st shirt is $\$5.995.$



    6. Determine the profit and the marginal profit from the sale of 1500 shirts.

      $P(x)=R(x)-C(x)=15x-\left( -0.005{{x}^{2}}+7.5x+200 \right)$

      $P(x)=0.0005{{x}^{2}}+7.5x-200$

      ${P}'(x)=0.001x+7.5$

      $P\left( 1500 \right)=\$12,175$

      ${P}'\left( 1500 \right)=\$9$

      The profit from the sale of 1500 shirts is \$12,175. The profit would be increased by \$9.00 if one more shirt is sold (1501 shirts sold).
